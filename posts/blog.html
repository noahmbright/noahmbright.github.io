Here I'll try to collect whatever I feel I understand well enough to share, so that maybe someone else can learn something as well.

The Game of Life in (Web)OpenGL. This implementation uses the standard ping-ponging tricks for rendering between a pair of textures and the canvas to simulate timesteps. The "novelty" here is the usage of every single byte in the textures, which taught me more than I could have imagined about textures, their format, and how to render to them.

RISC-V Interpreter. A summary of much of what I learned in a graduate level computer architecture class. This was our final project, so I only present a high level overview without any code.
